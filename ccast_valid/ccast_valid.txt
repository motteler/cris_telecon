motteler 1:59 PM
See slack/cris 14 Oct 2023 for a similar comparison with unapodized
radiances.  These tell a different story.  The low frequency biases
are still there, but partly masked by larger high frequency diffs,
with UMBC the outlier.  That's almost certainly because UW and NOAA
use an "SA first" algorithm, while UMBC uses a "ratio first" method.
The latter allows for better cancellation of instrument
responsivity.  UW and NOAA validated their alogrithms by comparison
with reference truth convolved with instrument responsivity, while
UMBC validated with a plain sinc basis.  This was verified in
calibration presentations, back in 2016.  Thus while UMBC is the
outlier, it is almost certainly the the better value.

I'm pretty sure these results subsume smaller differences, like from
the polarization correction.
